# Entry And Exit Strategy

## Objective

Convert a good stock thesis into a repeatable trade plan.

Core rule:

`selection finds the right stock, execution determines whether the gain is captured`

## Step 1. Identify The Trade Species

Before choosing a buy point, classify the stock:

- `persistent trend leader`
- `high-beta momentum trend`
- `event-driven revaluation`
- `mania / squeeze`

Do not use the same buy and sell logic for all four.

## Step 2. Entry Logic

### A. Breakout Entry

Prefer when:

- price is above `50DMA`
- ideally above `200DMA`
- price breaks a meaningful `4-6 month` base or prior high
- volume expands
- relative strength versus peers is improving

Best for:

- persistent trend leaders
- high-beta momentum trends

### B. Pullback Entry

Prefer when:

- a breakout has already happened
- price pulls back orderly into `20DMA` or `50DMA`
- volume contracts on the pullback
- the stock holds above the breakout area

Best for:

- persistent trend leaders
- cleaner momentum trends

### C. Catalyst Confirmation Entry

Prefer when:

- a hard catalyst confirms the thesis
- the stock gaps or re-rates but then holds the move
- the first post-catalyst base remains constructive

Best for:

- event-driven revaluations
- biotech, turnaround, major earnings-reset, and contract-win names

## Step 3. Entry Rules

Avoid framing entries as perfect bottoms.

Prefer:

- confirmation over anticipation
- structure over cheapness
- buying strength with context rather than buying weakness blindly

Red flags:

- price still below `50DMA`
- breakout without volume
- one-day news spike with no follow-through
- stock already extremely stretched above `50DMA`

## Step 4. Add Rules

Add only when the trade is proving itself.

Good add points:

- first tight consolidation after breakout
- clean hold of `20DMA` or `50DMA`
- post-earnings or post-catalyst continuation
- new high after controlled pullback

Bad add points:

- emotional chase after several vertical days
- adding simply because the stock is up
- adding into obvious climax behavior

## Step 5. Exit Rules

Use three exit categories.

### A. Thesis Exit

Sell when:

- the catalyst path breaks
- the earnings or data path invalidates the thesis
- the company loses leader status
- financing or dilution risk damages the core setup

### B. Technical Exit

Sell or reduce when:

- repeated failed breakouts appear
- the stock decisively loses `50DMA` after a mature uptrend
- recovery attempts become weak and short-lived

### C. Extension Exit

Trim when:

- price is far above `50DMA`
- the move becomes climactic
- sentiment gets much hotter than fundamentals

## Step 6. No-Chase Rules

Be careful when:

- the stock is `60%+` above `50DMA`
- several `10%+` days have already occurred in sequence
- the move is mostly float mechanics, not durable sponsorship
- likely dilution, unlock, or financing is near

## Step 7. Order-Execution Notes

- Prefer limit orders over blind market orders in volatile names.
- Treat stop orders carefully in fast markets because execution can occur materially away from the stop level.
- For thin or highly volatile stocks, execution quality matters almost as much as the thesis.
