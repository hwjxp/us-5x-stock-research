# Technical Setups

## Core Observation From Historical 5x Winners

The strongest winners usually did **not** begin as random dead-cat bounces.

A recurring sequence appears:

1. reclaim `50DMA`
2. reclaim `200DMA`
3. break out from a multi-week or multi-month base
4. expand volume on breakout
5. spend most of the major uptrend above `50DMA`

## Historical Summary

From the studied sample of historical U.S. 5x-style winners:

- effective breakouts consistently occurred above `50DMA`
- breakouts with valid `200DMA` history also tended to happen above `200DMA`
- breakout-day volume often expanded materially versus the recent average
- many winners still suffered severe in-year drawdowns, so correct position sizing mattered

## Four Chart Archetypes

### 1. Persistent Trend Leader

Representative names:

- `APP 2024`
- `WGS 2024`
- `DAVE 2024`

Traits:

- stays above `50DMA` for long stretches
- pullbacks are relatively controlled
- trend quality matters more than single-day fireworks

Best use:

- buy the first high-quality breakout or first constructive pullback

### 2. High-Beta Momentum Trend

Representative names:

- `TNDM 2018`
- `ENPH 2019`
- `CVNA 2023`
- `SEZL 2024`

Traits:

- strong breakouts from long bases
- repeated fast runs and hard pullbacks
- `50DMA` often acts as the primary reference

Best use:

- breakout buy, then add on orderly pullbacks or tight consolidations

### 3. Event-Driven Revaluation

Representative names:

- `NVAX 2020`
- `NIO 2020`
- `PSIX 2024`
- `SOUN 2024`

Traits:

- catalyst-heavy
- frequent gaps and sudden range expansion
- may reprice faster than a standard trend trader expects

Best use:

- buy close to catalyst confirmation or the first post-catalyst base

### 4. Mania / Squeeze

Representative names:

- `HIVE 2017`
- `RIOT 2017`
- `AMC 2021`
- `GME 2021`
- `RGTI 2024`

Traits:

- extreme volatility
- huge distance from `50DMA`
- frequent squeeze bars, gap risk, and violent mean reversion

Best use:

- treat as a different species; use smaller size and faster risk controls

## Execution Checklist

Prefer:

- reclaim of `200DMA`
- fresh range breakout
- volume expansion
- leadership versus sector or benchmark
- pullbacks that hold above `20DMA` or `50DMA`

Be careful when:

- price is already `60%+` above `50DMA`
- the entire move depends on squeeze mechanics
- the stock cannot hold any breakout for more than a few sessions
- there is heavy dilution risk right after the breakout

## Sell / Trim Clues

- repeated failed breakouts
- decisive loss of `50DMA` after a mature trend
- climax runs with huge distance above `50DMA`
- broken catalyst path or guidance reset
