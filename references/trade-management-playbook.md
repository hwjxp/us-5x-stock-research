# Trade Management Playbook

## Objective

Manage the position after entry without turning every fluctuation into a decision crisis.

## Stage Model

### Stage 1. Early Turn

What it looks like:

- price reclaims key moving averages
- first valid breakout appears
- the story is improving but not consensus

Default action:

- probe or start position
- define invalidation
- do not over-size

### Stage 2. Confirmation

What it looks like:

- breakout holds
- pullbacks are controlled
- volume confirms on upswings
- peers begin to respond

Default action:

- add into constructive setups
- raise conviction only if the structure earns it

### Stage 3. Expansion

What it looks like:

- trend persists above `20DMA` or `50DMA`
- market recognizes the story
- analysts, institutions, or momentum capital join

Default action:

- hold the core unless the structure deteriorates
- avoid over-trading strong trends

### Stage 4. Euphoria

What it looks like:

- price gets very extended
- media attention surges
- intraday volatility expands
- gains accelerate faster than fundamentals

Default action:

- trim in pieces
- tighten review
- do not add aggressively

### Stage 5. Breakdown Or Rotation

What it looks like:

- key averages fail
- breakouts stop working
- relative strength weakens
- capital rotates elsewhere

Default action:

- reduce, exit, or fully reset the trade

## Management Questions

Ask repeatedly:

- Is the thesis getting stronger, weaker, or just more crowded?
- Is this normal volatility for the stock type?
- Is the trend healthy or now purely emotional?
- If I were flat today, would I still want a position here?

## Common Mistakes

- selling a leader too early just because it feels expensive
- adding biggest size at the most euphoric point
- treating squeeze names like institutional trend names
- confusing a normal pullback with thesis failure
- ignoring obvious character change after climax behavior

## Output Template

When the user asks for active management, return:

- `Current stage`
- `What the chart is saying`
- `What the thesis is saying`
- `Action now`
- `What would trigger the next action`
