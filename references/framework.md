# Framework

## Objective

This framework is designed to find `5x-10x` U.S. stock winners, not to optimize for average portfolio smoothness.

The sequence is:

1. `Regime`
2. `Theme`
3. `Leader`
4. `Catalyst`
5. `Market-cap / float fit`
6. `Technical setup`
7. `Risk plan`

## 1. Regime

Ask first:

- Is liquidity expanding or tightening?
- Are rates falling, stable, or rising?
- Is the market rewarding duration, cyclicals, defensives, turnarounds, or narrative?
- Are 5x stocks currently clustering in one or two themes?

Working rule:

- In supportive liquidity regimes, more narrative-rich and early-stage names can work.
- In hostile rate or credit regimes, 5x density falls sharply and only unusually strong catalysts tend to survive.

## 2. Theme

A valid 5x theme usually has at least one of:

- very large TAM expansion
- major penetration shift
- severe supply-demand imbalance
- regulatory or approval event chain
- platform or infrastructure status

Questions:

- Is this a durable theme or a temporary trading slogan?
- Can leaders capture outsized economics, not just attention?
- Is capital already flowing into the space?

## 3. Leader

Prefer leader or clear strong number two.

Score these:

- product advantage
- distribution or platform advantage
- pricing power
- margin trajectory
- balance-sheet quality
- management credibility
- institutional sponsorship potential

## 4. Catalyst

No catalyst, no re-rating.

Common catalyst types:

- earnings inflection
- guidance reset
- clinical data or FDA milestones
- major product launch
- debt exchange or refinancing
- regulatory approval
- commodity price shift
- major customer or contract wins

## 5. Market-Cap / Float Fit

### Preferred 5x hunting zones

- `$500M-$5B`: high upside, high risk
- `$5B-$30B`: best balance for many practical 5x studies

### Hard filters

- enough average dollar volume to trade realistically
- no immediate existential financing problem unless the user specifically wants distressed turnarounds
- avoid treating tiny float distortions as durable alpha

## 6. Risk

Every output should specify:

- what would invalidate the thesis
- what kind of stock this is: steady trend, event-driven, squeeze-prone, or cyclical
- whether risk comes from business failure, financing, valuation, or pure sentiment collapse

## 7. Scoring Template

Use a `1-5` scale for each:

- `Regime Fit`
- `Theme Strength`
- `Leader Quality`
- `Catalyst Strength`
- `Market-Cap Fit`
- `Technical Setup`
- `Risk/Reward`

Suggested interpretation:

- `28-35`: elite setup, deserves serious attention
- `22-27`: interesting, but needs one or two things to improve
- `15-21`: mostly watchlist
- `<15`: not a 5x-style setup under this framework
