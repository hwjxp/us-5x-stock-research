# Earnings Playbook

## Objective

Handle earnings as a structured event, not a gambling ritual.

## Pre-Earnings Checklist

Before earnings, define:

- what the market already expects
- whether the stock is crowded long, crowded short, or balanced
- whether the thesis depends on the quarter itself or on guidance
- whether the setup is already extended

Questions:

- Is the company expected to beat, or is it expected to change the forward story?
- Is this a stock where guidance matters more than current-quarter numbers?
- Is the chart in a base, in a breakout, or in euphoria?

## Three Pre-Earnings Setups

### 1. Hold Through Earnings

More appropriate when:

- conviction is high
- the stock is a core name
- the catalyst is fundamental to the thesis
- position size is already calibrated for event risk

### 2. Reduce Before Earnings

More appropriate when:

- the stock is heavily extended
- expectations look extreme
- the position has already become oversized
- this is a squeeze-prone or story-driven name

### 3. Wait For Post-Earnings Reaction

More appropriate when:

- the setup is interesting but unproven
- there is high uncertainty on the result
- you want confirmation instead of anticipation

## What To Watch After Earnings

Focus in this order:

1. guidance
2. backlog / pipeline / bookings / demand indicators
3. margin trajectory
4. operating cash flow or burn
5. market reaction versus the reported numbers

The reaction often tells more than the headline.

## Good Post-Earnings Action

Consider buying or adding when:

- results are strong and the stock holds the gap
- guidance improves and sellers cannot push it back into the old range
- a first post-earnings base forms above support

## Bad Post-Earnings Action

Be careful when:

- the company beats but the stock cannot hold gains
- numbers look strong but guidance disappoints
- the move is only a one-day spike with no follow-through

## Decision Template

- `Expectation before earnings`
- `What actually happened`
- `What the market reaction says`
- `Action now`
- `Invalidation after earnings`
